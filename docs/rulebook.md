## Project Requirements

### Core requirement

Artificial intelligence or machine learning must be a central and meaningful component of your project; not a peripheral feature. A project that merely calls an API without any meaningful integration, customization, or application to a problem will not be considered compliant.

### Originality & development window

- The project must be original work as per this rulebook.
- Your GitHub repository must show commits made during the competition window (May 14–July 8, 2026). Repositories with no commits in this period will be flagged.

### Permitted tools & technologies

- Any programming language, framework, or cloud platform is allowed.
- Use of pre-trained models, foundation models (e.g. GPT-4, Gemini, LLaMA), and fine-tuned models is fully permitted and encouraged.
- Public APIs (OpenAI, Hugging Face, Google AI, etc.) are allowed. Usage costs are the team’s responsibility.
- Open-source libraries and datasets are allowed. All third-party resources must be properly
attributed.
- No-code/low-code AI tools (e.g. Bubble, FlutterFlow with AI plugins) are permitted for Track D only, provided the report clearly explains the AI logic and the team can answer technical
questions during judging.

### Live demo requirement

Your project must be deployed and accessible via a public URL. Judges will evaluate your product
directly in the browser. No local setup, installation, or login should be required to access the core
demo.

Accepted hosting platforms include (not limited to):

- Hugging Face Spaces — recommended for ML/Gradio/Streamlit apps
- Streamlit Community Cloud
- Vercel or Netlify — for frontend-heavy apps
- Render — for full-stack apps with a backend


## Competition Tracks

Teams must declare their track at submission form (will be provided to the teams after registration). You may not switch tracks after submission closes.

| Track   | Focus Area |
|---------|------------|
| Track A | Health & Society — Use AI to address a healthcare, mental health, or social welfare problem relevant to Bangladesh or beyond. |
| Track B | Environment & Sustainability — Build something addressing climate change, agriculture, disaster response, or environmental monitoring. |
| Track C | Education & Accessibility — AI tools that improve learning outcomes or make technology more inclusive for underserved populations. |
| Track D | Open Innovation — Any problem domain of the team’s choosing. AI must be central to the solution. Justify your problem choice in the report. |
| Track E | National Defence — AI tool that improves national security. |

## Judging Criteria
All submissions will be evaluated by a panel of at least two judges. Scores from all judges will be averaged. The organizing committee may moderate scores in case of significant
discrepancies.

| Criterion                         | Weight | Max Score |
|-----------------------------------|--------|-----------|
| Innovation & Originality          | 25%    | 25        |
| Technical Implementation          | 25%    | 25        |
| Real-world Impact & Relevance     | 20%    | 20        |
| Demo Quality & Functionality      | 20%    | 20        |
| Presentation & Communication      | 10%    | 10        |
| **Total**                         | **100%** | **100** |

## Evaluation notes

- Technical Implementation does not require training a model from scratch. Effective, appropriate, and well-integrated use of existing tools and APIs will score well.
- Demo Quality is evaluated on your live hosted URL and demo video. A broken demo will result in a significantly lower score for this criterion.
- Innovation is assessed relative to the problem context — a novel approach to a local problem can score higher than a technically complex but routine solution.
- Presentation score applies only to teams in the Final Day. Remote teams’ communication scores are based on the report and video.
